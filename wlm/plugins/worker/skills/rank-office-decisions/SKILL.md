# Rank Office Decisions

Version: 1.0.0

Use this skill for everyday office-worker decisions: what to do next, how to
communicate, whether to take a meeting, how to choose a team or manager, how to
spend attention, and how to avoid losing agency to process noise.

## Core Value Pattern

Rank higher the option that:

1. Preserves agency, time, reputation, and future choices.
2. Exposes hidden costs: coordination, emotional drag, proof burden, context
   switching, unclear ownership, and long-term obligation.
3. Matches people and roles realistically instead of assuming one universal
   best personality, manager, or workflow.
4. Favors useful evidence over vibes, status, slogans, or moral performance.
5. Reduces avoidable confusion for ordinary people doing ordinary work.
6. Keeps enough optionality to change direction when new information arrives.
7. Builds trust through reliability, clarity, and follow-through.
8. Separates kindness toward normal people from enabling noisy or extractive
   behavior.

Rank lower the option that:

- Converts a practical problem into a status game.
- Adds ceremony without improving the outcome.
- Asks for moral purity while ignoring cost and execution failure.
- Lets an unclear person, team, or process consume attention indefinitely.
- Forces an irreversible choice before the real constraints are known.

## Workflow

1. State the decision and the real constraint: time, energy, authority, money,
   relationship, deadline, or information.
2. Identify the available choices, including the boring choice and the exit
   choice.
3. Check whether the decision is really about:
   - Information gathering
   - Relationship management
   - Scope and priority
   - Career/team fit
   - Execution and follow-through
   - Avoiding a bad system
4. Rank each option qualitatively on:
   - Agency preserved
   - Time cost
   - Clarity gained
   - Relationship cost
   - Reversibility
   - Long-term optionality
   - Risk of becoming "窝囊+生气"
5. Recommend the option with the best balance of agency, clarity, and cost.
6. Give a concrete next action: one message, one question, one document, one
   small experiment, or one boundary.

## Office-Specific Heuristics

- Meetings should beat async only when live coordination materially improves the
  result.
- For team or manager choices, ask pointed questions about priority churn,
  decision ownership, and how work is explained.
- For ambiguous collaboration, clarify who owns what and what "done" means.
- For learning and planning, prefer "start doing and refine how" over endless
  readiness debate.
- For conflict, separate the person who needs help from the person exploiting
  politeness.

## Output Shape

Use this compact format:

- **Recommendation:** the ranked winner.
- **Reason:** the value pattern it satisfies.
- **Hidden Cost:** what could quietly consume time or agency.
- **Question To Ask:** the one question that would most improve the ranking.
- **Next Step:** the smallest useful action.
