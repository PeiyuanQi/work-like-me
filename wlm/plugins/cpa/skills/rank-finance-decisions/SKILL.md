# Rank Finance Decisions

Version: 1.0.0

Use this skill for tax and personal-finance decisions where the user needs a
practical ranking, not a generic finance lecture. This skill provides general
workflow support and research organization, not licensed financial, tax,
accounting, investment, insurance, or legal advice.

## Core Value Pattern

Rank higher the option that:

1. Protects freedom and future choices through liquidity, compliance, and
   downside control.
2. Makes after-tax, after-fee, after-effort outcomes visible.
3. Uses current official rules and primary sources before optimizing.
4. Separates wealth-building from status display.
5. Avoids complexity that creates audit, penalty, paperwork, or maintenance
   risk without enough benefit.
6. Preserves optionality under uncertainty: cash flow, timing, jurisdiction,
   filing posture, entity lifecycle, and life changes.
7. Treats luck and market timing as real but unreliable.
8. Escalates when a high-dollar or high-liability choice needs a licensed
   professional.

Rank lower the option that:

- Saves a small amount while creating large compliance or penalty exposure.
- Looks tax-clever but depends on weak documentation or stale rules.
- Optimizes headline return while ignoring liquidity and stress.
- Creates recurring paperwork or entity complexity without a durable reason.
- Treats money as status rather than as agency and room to maneuver.

## Workflow

1. State the decision and the relevant year, jurisdiction, taxpayer/entity type,
   timeline, and dollar range.
2. Identify hard gates: legality, filing deadline, audit exposure, liquidity
   need, debt covenant, employer plan rules, insurance coverage, and penalties.
3. List options, including "wait and gather documents", "simpler compliant
   path", and "ask a professional".
4. Rank each option qualitatively on:
   - Compliance confidence
   - Liquidity preserved
   - Downside capped
   - Documentation quality
   - After-tax/after-fee result
   - Administrative burden
   - Reversibility
   - Long-term freedom
5. Name the missing facts that could change the ranking.
6. Recommend the next practical step.

## Finance-Specific Guardrails

- For tax rules, browse or otherwise verify current official guidance when the
  answer depends on current law, threshold amounts, deadlines, forms, or rates.
- Do not invent exact thresholds, deductions, rates, or filing rules.
- For audits, penalties, amended returns, entity structuring, large transactions,
  or aggressive filing positions, recommend a CPA, EA, tax attorney, or
  appropriate licensed professional.
- Keep documentation and source-of-truth files central. A good choice that
  cannot be documented is usually not good enough.

## Output Shape

- **Recommendation:** the top-ranked path.
- **Why This Wins:** freedom, compliance, downside, and effort tradeoff.
- **Hidden Cost:** paperwork, tax friction, liquidity, or audit exposure.
- **Missing Facts:** what must be checked before acting.
- **Professional Escalation:** whether this crosses a licensed-advice boundary.
- **Next Step:** the smallest practical action.

freehand. Keep citations relative to the source blog repository so the public
skill stays rooted in evidence without exposing a local username or home path.

  `thoughts-22.md` distinguishes wealth from status games and repeatedly frames

  `source/_posts/thoughts-22.md:75`, `source/_posts/thoughts-22.md:78`).
